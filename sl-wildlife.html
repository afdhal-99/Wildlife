About Sri Lankan wildlife







Sri Lanka boasts a rich tapestry of wildlife beyond the renowned Yala and Wilpattu National Parks. The Sinharaja Forest Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a verdant
haven for biodiversity, hosting an array of endemic species. Horton Plains National Park captivates with its dramatic landscapes, featuring grassy plateaus and the iconic
World's End precipice. Udawalawe National Park stands out for its thriving elephant population, offering a unique safari experience. Bundala National Park,
a Ramsar Wetland site, attracts bird enthusiasts with its diverse avian residents. These lesser-explored gems contribute to
Sri Lanka's status as a biodiversity hotspot, inviting nature enthusiasts to discover the country's wild wonders.







Sinharaja-Forest







Sinharaja Forest, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Sri Lanka, is a biodiversity hotspot renowned for its rich and unique ecosystems. Spread across 11,187 hectares, this tropical rainforest is a treasure trove of endemic flora and fauna. Home to a myriad of species, including vibrant birds, elusive mammals, and exotic insects, Sinharaja is a haven for nature enthusiasts and researchers alike. The dense canopy, interspersed with crystal-clear streams and cascading waterfalls, creates a mesmerizing landscape. As one of the last remaining expanses of undisturbed rainforest in Sri Lanka, Sinharaja plays a crucial role in preserving the island nation's natural heritage
